Education & Career Timeline

2009

YouTube Channel Launch

High School Years

Started the MKBHD YouTube channel while attending South Brunswick High School in New Jersey. Initially created videos about consumer technology and gadget reviews. The channel name originated from his initials (MKB) and "HD" for high-definition, reflecting his focus on video quality from the beginning.

2011-2015

Higher Education

Quinnipiac University

Attended Quinnipiac University in Hamden, Connecticut, where he studied Business and played on the university's Ultimate Frisbee team. Continued to grow his YouTube channel while balancing academics and athletics. During this period, his channel gained significant traction, reaching 100,000 subscribers by 2012.

2014

Professional Transition

Full-Time YouTuber

After graduating from Quinnipiac University, Brownlee decided to pursue YouTube full-time. His channel had already gained significant traction, making this a viable career choice. He moved to New York City to focus on content creation and began collaborating with other tech YouTubers.

2015-Present

Rise to Prominence

Industry Recognition

Gained recognition as one of the most influential tech reviewers. Started working with major tech companies, attending exclusive launch events, and interviewing tech industry leaders. His channel reached 1 million subscribers in 2015 and continued exponential growth.

2018

Waveform Podcast

Content Expansion

Launched the Waveform podcast with Andrew Manganelli, expanding his content beyond YouTube videos to in-depth discussions about technology, business, and industry trends. The podcast became popular for its technical depth and industry insights.

2020

Streamy Awards

Industry Recognition

Won the Streamy Award for Best Technology Channel for two consecutive years (2019 and 2020), solidifying his position as a leading tech content creator. Also received the Shorty Award for Best in Science and Technology in 2018.

2021

Studio Expansion

Production Upgrade

Invested in a state-of-the-art studio setup, including 8K cameras and professional lighting equipment. This allowed for even higher production quality and set new standards for tech review videos on YouTube.
